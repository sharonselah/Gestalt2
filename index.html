The Gestalt Principles

A website showing the Gestalt principles of similiarity, continuation, closure, proximity, and figure and ground.

Similarity

In gestalt, similar elements are visually grouped, regardless of their proximity to each other. They can be grouped by color, shape, or size.

Continuation

The law of continuity posits that the human eye will follow the smoothest path when viewing lines, regardless of how the lines were actually drawn.

Closure

If enough of the shape is indicated, people perceive the whole by filling in the missing information

Proximity

Proximity occurs when elements are placed close together. Grouping objects into a single area can also have a strong proximity effect.

Figure and Ground

Figure refers to That what is the important subject. Also known as Foreground. Ground denotes That what is the environment of the subject. Also known as Background

Focal Point

The focal point principle states that whatever stands out visually will capture and hold the viewer's attention first.
